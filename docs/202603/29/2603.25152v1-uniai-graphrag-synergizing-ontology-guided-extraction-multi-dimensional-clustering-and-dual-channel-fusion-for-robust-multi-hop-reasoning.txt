Abstract 

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems [ 1] face significant challenges in complex reasoning, multi-hop queries, and domain-specific QA. While existing GraphRAG [ 2 ,3, 4] frameworks have made progress in structural knowledge organization [ 5], they still have limitations in cross-industry adaptability, community report integrity, and retrieval performance. This paper proposes UniAI-GraphRAG, an enhanced framework built upon open-source GraphRAG. The framework introduces three core innovations: (1) Ontology-Guided Knowledge Extraction [6 ] that uses predefined Schema to guide LLMs in accurately identifying domain-specific entities and relations; (2) Multi-Dimensional Community Clustering Strategy that improves community completeness through alignment completion, attribute-based clustering, and multi-hop relationship clustering; (3) Dual-Channel Graph Retrieval Fusion that balances QA accuracy and performance through hybrid graph and community retrieval. Evaluation results on MultiHopRAG [7] benchmark show that UniAI-GraphRAG outperforms mainstream open source solutions (e.g., LightRAG [ 8]) in comprehensive F1 scores [ 9], particularly in inference and temporal queries. The code is available at https://github. com/UnicomAI/wanwu/tree/main/rag/rag_ open_source/rag_core/graph .

1 Introduction 

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demon-strated remarkable capabilities in natural language understanding and generation. However, they still suffer from hallucination and lack of domain-specific knowledge, particularly in vertical indus-tries such as healthcare, finance, and legal domains. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) addresses these limitations by grounding LLM responses in external knowledge sources. GraphRAG extends traditional RAG by or-ganizing fragmented knowledge into structured graphs, enhancing complex reasoning capabilities. Despite its promise, existing GraphRAG frame-works face critical limitations in domain adaptabil-ity, community clustering, and retrieval efficiency for vertical applications. To address these challenges, we propose UniAI-GraphRAG, an enhanced framework with the following contributions: • Precise Industry Extraction: Introduce an 

Ontology guidance mechanism using prede-fined schema templates as part of the prompt. This ensures that the generated knowledge graph aligns strictly with industry logic and reduces noise. • Comprehensive Community Indexing: Im-plement a multi-dimensional community clus-tering strategy, including post-processing to fix broken edges. This supports clustering by specific attributes (such as time or location) and multi-hop relationships to strengthen com-plex reasoning chains. • Real-time Dual-Channel Retrieval: Estab-lish a hybrid retrieval architecture consisting of two specialized paths: 

– Channel 1 (Graph Retrieval): Focuses on local precision through dynamic en-tity traversal and attribute matching to locate factual details. 

– Channel 2 (Community Reports): Fo-cuses on global summarization by match-ing query themes with multi-dimensional community topics for macro-level in-sights.

2 Background 

By organizing fragmented knowledge into struc-tured graphs, GraphRAG enhances the complex reasoning capabilities of LLMs[ 10 , 11 ]. However, three core bottlenecks currently limit its perfor-mance in vertical fields: • Insufficient "Domain Adaptability" in Extraction: Most existing methods use "Schema-Free" extraction. In professional fields like medicine or finance, this leads to low entity recognition rates, blurred seman-tics, and excessive noise. Without Ontology constraints, the generated graph structure is loose[ 12 ], making complex reasoning chains difficult to maintain. 

– Construction Issue: Lacking onto-logical guidance, generated graphs ex-hibit loose structures, failing to encode domain-specific hierarchies and logic. 

– Retrieval Issue: Unreliable graph qual-ity prevents the exploitation of structural cues, causing failures in complex multi-hop reasoning. 

– Critical Issue: A fundamental misalign-ment exists between generic construc-tion methods and vertical domain needs, necessitating excessive manual interven-tion for domain adaptation. • Single-Dimensional Limitations in Cluster-ing: Algorithms like Leiden or Louvain focus primarily on topological connectivity[ 2, 13 ], ignoring semantic relevance. This results in broken structures where cross-community edges are severed, and a lack of dynamic ag-gregation for business attributes like time or location. 

– Structural Fragmentation: Non-overlapping clustering severs inter-community links, causing information loss and incomplete summaries. 

– Dimensional Rigidity: Topology-only clustering lacks support for dynamic, attribute-based aggregation (time, loca-tion), hindering generalization in tempo-ral and multi-dimensional tasks. 

– Retrieval Blind Spots: Single-view com-munities cannot concurrently address micro-level fact-checking and macro-level trend analysis. • Time Latency in Retrieval: Traditional systems often use LLMs for online query rewriting[ 14 ] or decomposition, leading to high token costs and long latency.

UniAI-GraphRAG follows the Index-Retrieve-Generate paradigm with specific optimizations: • Indexing: Quality domain graph construction via ontology-guided extraction. • Retrieval: A dual-channel fusion mode that dynamically selects the optimal path. • Generation: Utilizing enhanced community reports and entity context for comprehensive answers. 3.1 Ontology-Guided Knowledge Extraction 

> Figure 2: Extraction

As shown in Figure 2, the framework supports domain expert-defined Schema templates. By in-putting these Schemas as prompts, the LLM is guided to extract SPO triples with high accuracy, effectively reducing noise and improving cross-domain migration. In the construction of knowl-edge graphs, an Ontology (O) is defined as a set of logical constraints governing domain concepts and their interrelations. We formalize the Ontology Schema as a triplet constraint space S = ( E, R, Φ) ,where: • E denotes the set of permissible entity types (e.g., Person , Organization ). • R represents the set of allowable relation types (e.g., EmployedBy , LocatedIn ). • Φ serves as the type constraint function, defin-ing the valid domain and range for triples: 

Φ( r) = ( dom (r), range (r)) , ∀r ∈ R (1) Here, dom (r) ⊆ E specifies the head en-tity type constraint for relation r, while range (r) ⊆ E specifies the tail entity type constraint. 3.2 Multi-Dimensional Community Cluster-ing Strategy

4 Experiments and Evaluation 

4.1 Dataset 

We conducted a comprehensive evaluation on the MultiHopRAG benchmark dataset. Multi-HopRAG is a high-quality dataset specifically de-signed to assess the multi-hop reasoning capabil-ities of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems[ 3, 16 ]. It focuses on evaluating logical rea-soning across multiple documents, temporal depen-dencies, and attribute comparisons between various facts. The dataset includes three distinct categories of queries designed to test multi-hop reasoning: • Inference Query: Requires logical derivation from multiple scattered facts. • Comparison Query: Involves horizontal comparison of event attributes. • Temporal Query: Requires understanding the chronological order and time dependen-cies of events. Detailed descriptions of these query types are provided in Table 1. 

4.2 Metrics 

• Relevancy: Correlation between retrieved content and the question. • Recall: Coverage of key facts. • F1 Score: The harmonic mean of Relevancy and Recall.

5 Discussion and Future Work 

5.1 Advantages 

• Domain Portability: By defining industry-specific ontologies, the system harmonizes do-main knowledge into unified business seman-tics. This allows LLMs to better grasp busi-ness logic, significantly enhancing the porta-bility of UniAI-GraphRAG across different sectors. • Augmented Community Clustering: Inte-grating completion mechanisms with multi-dimensional clustering improves report qual-ity and thematic richness, supporting multi-perspective retrieval that far exceeds tradi-tional summarization methods. • Dual-Channel Graph Fusion: Our dual-channel mode combines fine-grained entity relations with coarse-grained community se-mantics. This balances local and global search, maximizing graph-based enhance-ments without increasing retrieval latency compared to standard RAG. 5.2 Limitations 

• Expert-Dependent Schema Definition: The definition of industry ontologies and schemas relies heavily on manual expertise, leading to high human resource costs and limited scala-bility for rapid deployment. • Absence of Multimodal Support: The cur-rent framework only processes textual data, leaving multimodal assets (e.g., images and videos) unutilized within the knowledge graph and community reports. • Impact of Chunking Strategies: Since ex-traction is performed on pre-defined chunks, the semantic fragmentation inherent in tradi-tional segmentation can compromise the in-tegrity of knowledge discovery. 

5.3 Future Work 

• Semi-Automated Schema Definition: We in-tend to implement prompt-driven mechanisms for automated schema pre-definition, followed by human-in-the-loop verification to reduce manual costs. • Multimodal Integration: Future versions will extend the schema to support cross-modal entity normalization, enabling a uni-fied GraphRAG architecture that handles mul-timodal inputs and outputs. • Adaptive Extraction Windows: By leverag-ing ReAct patterns and document-level hierar-chy, the system will adaptively adjust text seg-ment boundaries during extraction to ensure semantic completeness and mitigate chunking artifacts. 

6 Conclusion 

By integrating an ontology-guided knowledge ex-traction paradigm, multi-source knowledge integra-tion, and a multi-dimensional community cluster-ing mechanism, UniAI-GraphRAG innovatively addresses the critical challenges inherent in tra-ditional GraphRAG frameworks. These include insufficient domain adaptability in cross-industry applications, poor coverage of entity relationships, and the persistent trade-off between retrieval effi-ciency and accuracy. The core contributions of this work are sum-marized as follows: • Methodological Innovation: We propose a domain-specific KG construction and retrieval framework guided by structured schemas. By synergizing multi-dimensional community clustering with a dual-channel retrieval mode, the system maximizes the benefits of graph-based augmentation without incurring addi-tional latency, achieving a co-optimization of retrieval performance and precision. • Performance Superiority: Empirical evalua-tions on the MultiHop-RAG benchmark show that UniAI-GraphRAG surpasses the state-of-the-art open-source framework LightRAG by 

2.23% in integrated F1-score. Additionally, it provides a substantial 22.45% improvement in F1-score over the Dify Naive RAG baseline.
